What's a zine?

  • Zines are handmade, self-published booklets.
  • Origins trace back to science fiction fanzines of the 1930s.
  • Critical to activist and punk music movements 1960s/70s.
  • Photocopier (Xerox machine) is key. As printing and publishing technology became more accessible, zines have continually emerged from the desire for participatory knowledge making and community building.

What's a zine?

  • Sci-fi Fanzines (1930s): New Worlds
  • Riot Grrrl Zines (1990s): Bikini Kill and Jigsaw
  • Post-Digital Zine Culture (2000s - present): Brown Recluse Distro and Queer Zine Archive Project (QZAP)

Why didn't the computer kill zines?

As zine scholar Alison Piepmeir observes, unlike blogs and other forms of digital media, zines are not template driven. Zines, she writes, call forth ”[t]he necessity of making aesthetic decisions” and manipulating materials — paper, pencils, scissors, glue, clippings from magazines, photographs, photocopier. The “[t]he personal, physical involvement” of making these choices imbues zines with “not only intentionality but also care” (221).

Why make zines?

  • make visible social/cultural identities
  • participatory literacy and culture
  • materiality, making, and craft
  • ("old" and "new"") technology and writing
  • histories of composing practices
